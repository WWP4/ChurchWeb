The Problem

Most businesses do not have a revenue problem. They have a timing problem.

Cash stress often comes from when money moves, not just how much comes in. When that timing is unclear, good businesses feel tight for no obvious reason.

Receivables come in late

Clients pay on their schedule while payroll, vendors, and taxes stay fixed.

Costs stack at the wrong time

Inventory, debt, or large bills can hit at the same moment and create pressure fast.

Reports do not tell the full story

A business can look fine on paper and still feel strained week to week.
